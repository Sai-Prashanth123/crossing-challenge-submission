## My approach, in one paragraph

I built two separate models bundled into one `model.pkl`. For **intent** (will the pedestrian cross?), I trained a LightGBM classifier on 33 features I engineered from the tracklet — geometry, dynamics, ego-pedestrian relative motion, scene categoricals, and position priors. Cross-validation is grouped by `ped_id` to avoid leakage. After training, I calibrated the output probabilities with isotonic regression on out-of-fold predictions — this matters because BCE is the loss, and miscalibrated probabilities hurt it directly. I did **not** use class weights to handle the 7% positive imbalance; that made calibration worse, not better. For **trajectory** (where will they be at +0.5, +1, +1.5, +2 s?), I trained a small GRU (~200K parameters, 2 layers, hidden 128) that predicts the **error** of a constant-velocity guess at each horizon — so it only has to learn the parts the simple guess gets wrong. Bbox width and height are held constant at the current-frame size. Loss is SmoothL1 instead of MSE, because long-horizon residuals are heavy-tailed and MSE squashes that into noise. The two heads are not jointly trained: tabular and sequence have different optima, and decoupling let me iterate intent on a laptop while the GRU trained on Colab T4.

## What I tried that didn't work

- **First GRU run** used MSE loss, hidden=64, and ego-speed augmentation. ADE landed at 34 px — *worse* than constant velocity at +1.5/2.0 s. Diagnosis: MSE squared the heavy-tailed long-horizon residuals into noise, the model was undersized, and augmentation added noise without real signal. Switching to SmoothL1, doubling hidden to 128, and dropping the augmentation got me from 34 → 28.75 px in one retrain.
- **Class weights `{0:1, 1:5}`** on the LightGBM head pushed positive-class scores higher and broke calibration even with isotonic correction afterward. BCE went 0.21 → 0.22. Removed; let LightGBM train on the natural ~7% positive rate.
- **Predicting bbox width and height** alongside center positions added 8 outputs, more noise, and almost no ADE gain. Reverted to constant size.

## Where AI tooling sped me up most

I used **Claude Code** (Anthropic's CLI) throughout. Highest-leverage uses:

- **Spec-first workflow** (brainstorm → design doc → implementation plan → code). The structured review caught two bugs *before* any code: a test was using the Eval baseline as a Dev regression gate; the spec said 30 features while the code allocated 33.
- **Defensive `predict.py`.** Every safety clamp (bboxes to `[-2000, 4000]`, intent to `[1e-6, 1-1e-6]`, NaN → frame center) was generated by reading `grade.py` directly. My local score and the grader's score agree to four digits.
- **Diagnostic retraining.** When the first GRU was 34 px, Claude listed three named hypotheses (heavy-tailed residuals → SmoothL1; capacity → hidden 128; augmentation muddying signal → drop). All three landed correctly.
- **Fast scaffolding.** The 33-feature builder, the GRU training loop with cosine warmup and best-checkpoint saving, and the test files took minutes, not hours.

Where it didn't help: the **modeling intuition**. Decisions like *predict residuals over constant velocity* (the standard PIE/JAAD trick), *hold bbox size constant*, and *augment ego-speed for the sidewalk-robot distribution shift* (called out in the FAQ) came from reading the brief and the papers carefully. Claude was a fast pair, not the architect.

## Next experiments

If I kept going, in priority order:

1. **Per-horizon loss weighting** — SmoothL1 currently treats +0.5 s and +2.0 s equally, but the points are at long horizons.
2. **Body-pose features** from a pretrained MoveNet or PoseNet. Keypoint angles correlate strongly with crossing intent. Costs ~30 ms latency and a bigger image; would need to fit under the 2 GB cap carefully.
3. **5-fold GRU ensemble** — reliable 5–10% ADE reduction, overnight on Colab.
4. **Test-time blend of constant-velocity and GRU per horizon**, weight learned on Dev. Hedges out-of-distribution scenes where the GRU drifts.
5. **Domain-shift handling.** Currently just synthetic ego-speed augmentation. Better: sample-reweight toward low-speed PIE/JAAD segments, or annotate a small robot-domain set.
